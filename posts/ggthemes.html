ggplot 2 and ggthemes

Data science is one of those buzzwords with an a somewhat vague definition. It’s been described as having multiple entry points, with each path leading to the intersection of a venn diagram, connecting computer science, statistics, social science, and other disciplines.

This will most certainly be the subject of a future blog post, but for the time being, a more workable definition of data science should be something more along the lines of “making sense of data”. And for this more concise definition, we have a ton of tools in R to do exactly that.

I want to introduce readers to two R packages that will make visualizing data easy and beautiful. The (1) ggplot2 library in R gives us the ability to visualize data, in an aesthetically pleasing way, in just a few lines of code, while the (2) ggthemes library will allow us to add on a number of premade “themes” to our plot, producing publication ready plots in a matter of seconds.

As an example, I’ve downloaded a dataset from kaggle - a platform for predictive modelling and analytics competitions on which companies and researchers post their data and statisticians and data miners from all over the world compete to produce the best models. This particular data set is one of the most comprehensive US homicide datasets available to the public.
